About Me

"Behavioral cognition is an important aspect of our understanding of neuroscience."

I am currently studying at the School of Automation, Northwestern Polytechnical University. My research focuses on multi-animal pose estimation and behavior recognition, primarily using marmosets as the main subjects.

In multi-animal pose estimation, occlusion among subjects is a major challenge faced by current algorithms. Pose estimation serves as the foundation for skeleton-based behavior recognition, and these two tasks complement each other.
